# Resume Revision Notes

## Overall Direction

Revise the resume in three directions:

- Make it look more current.
- Make the strongest work easier to scan.
- Make the side projects do more strategic work.

The current resume reads primarily as "senior PHP engineer with infra experience," but the public portfolio and GitHub show a broader and more modern story:

- PWAs
- TypeScript / Express
- SQLite
- privacy-focused apps
- offline support
- barcode scanning
- encryption
- MCP work

That gap is worth fixing.

## Highest-Value Revisions

- Replace the current summary with one that leads on scope and product outcomes, not just stack.
- Add a short `Selected Projects` section.
- Remove or demote weaker vanity-style bullets.
- Tighten the Education section.
- Tailor the skills section toward hiring keywords that are actually demonstrated publicly.

## Specific Content Changes

- In the ETA section, add the stack to 1-2 bullets.
- Add one bullet showing system ownership, not just implementation.
- Add one bullet that speaks to product judgment or cross-functional leadership.
- Swap one older Steward Goods bullet for a stronger recent personal-project bullet from GitHub.
- Consider renaming `Technical Skills` to `Core Technologies`.

## What To Cut Or Compress

- Education can likely be 2 lines.
- Some skill categories are broad enough that they read padded.
- The Steward Goods section can probably be shortened by one bullet if selected projects are added.
- The ETA section should stay prominent, but every bullet should show measurable impact, architectural ownership, or uncommon technical depth.
